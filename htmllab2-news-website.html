Bali G20 Summit Starts

By 2030, half of India's electricity will be generated from renewable sources, says PM Modi

The 2022 G20 Bali summit is the seventeenth meeting of Group of Twenty (G20), a summit scheduled to take place in Nusa Dua, Bali, Indonesia on 15–16 November 2022.Indonesia's presidency began on 1 December 2021, leading up to the summit in the fourth quarter of 2022. The presidency handover ceremony was held as an intimate event, in which the G20 Presidency Gavel was transferred from Italian Prime Minister Mario DraghiMario Draghi to Indonesian president Joko Widodo at the close of the Rome summit.

The Indonesian government budgeted Rp 674 billion (~USD 45 million) for the G20 events.[5] For the event's security, the Indonesian government deployed around 10,000 police officers and 18,000 soldiers, including 6,000 soldiers from Kodam IX/Udayana headquartered in Bali.[6] Shortly before, during, and after the summit, flights to Bali's I Gusti Ngurah Rai International Airport became restricted, with limited operating hours for commercial aircraft and a ban against commercial aircraft staying overnight. Some flights are redirected to nearby airports in Surabaya, Lombok, and Makassar.
